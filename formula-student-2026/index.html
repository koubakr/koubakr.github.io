Formula Student 2026

Project is still ongoing.

Formula Student Head of Electronics: Leading the end-to-end architectural design of the 2026 competition vehicle, transitioning from prototype concepts to high-reliability production systems. Key implementations include a DTA Fast ECU, RapidHarness for professional loom design, custom PCBs for power distribution, and a Raspberry Pi interface for real-time CAN bus telemetry.

As the Head of Electronic Systems for the Swansea University Race Engineering (SURE) team for the 2026 Formula Student UK competition, I am responsible for the design and implementation of the vehicle's car electronics systems.

My primary focus this season is integrating a new ECU as well as design and manufacturing of a new wire harness. We are also focusing on more sensors on the car to collect more data while testing, as well as refining our drive by wire and flat shifting system. We also aim to make a custom fuse box and implement a new dashboard and steering wheel system.

ECU and wire harness

We upgraded the Electronic Control Unit to a DTA Fast T8+ ECU while retaining our proven sensors. To ensure easy manufacturing and documentation, the wiring harness was designed using RapidHarness. The professional documentation provided by the software streamlined the manufacturing and installation process, resulting in a flawless engine start-up and successful track validation.

We are executing a two-version wiring strategy. Version 1 (pictured below) was optimized for flexibility, enabling us to easily modify connections as we integrated new systems during the first semester. The upcoming Version 2 is the 'production' standard, made for maximum durability and reliability to withstand the wear of multiple racing seasons.

To optimize engine performance, we incorporated MegaLogViewer for post-run analysis. Using real-world data logs, we fine-tuned the ignition and fuel tables to match track conditions, known as road-dyno. This calibration eliminated engine roughness, increased power delivery, and improved fuel efficiency, resulting in a highly reliable powertrain, all without the need for a expensive static dyno tuning run.

Fuse and relay box

We have upgraded the vehicle's fuse box to a custom PCB-based solution. This design prioritizes high-current handling and mechanical robustness suitable for track conditions. By utilizing standard, interchangeable automotive relays and fuses, the system ensures reliable connections while remaining easy to service and maintain.

BSPD device

This BSPD utilizes discrete logic to ensure safe vehicle shutdown during simultaneous braking and acceleration. This year’s iteration upgrades the original schematic by employing SMD components and professional PCB manufacturing. This shift from prototype methods to industry-standard fabrication ensures maximum reliability for this critical safety component.

The design is still ongoing.

Electronic shifting

We are refining the electronic shifting setup to prioritize reliability. The system utilizes a high-performance solenoid driven by ECU-controlled relays. Current testing focuses on integrating these shift parameters with the Drive-By-Wire system. This setup is key to achieving the seamless and instantaneous shifting required for optimal track performance, as well as safe usage under competition conditions.

The design is still ongoing.

Dashboard

A easy-to-understand screen behind the steering wheel showing live data about the car, using a Raspberry Pi and CAN bus.

The design is still ongoing.
