Acoustic Levitation

Project is still ongoing.

Ultrasonic Phased-Array Levitator: An embedded system capable of levitating and manipulating matter in mid-air using real-time acoustic beamforming, powered by an STM32 microcontroller and shift registers on a custom-made PCB. 

For my undergraduate dissertation, I engineered an acoustic levitation system based on the principles of ultrasonic standing waves. The core objective was to generate stable pressure nodes—pockets of low pressure created by constructive and destructive interference—capable of trapping and levitating light objects in mid-air.

Unlike simple static levitators, this system employs a phased-array approach. By controlling the phase delay of individual emitters, the system creates a focused "beam" of sound, known as beamforming.

  • Custom transducer array using Murata MA40S4S (40kHz) speakers driven by MIC4127 MOSFET drivers.
  • STM32 G474 microcontroller implementing real-time phase generation.
  • SN74HC59N shift registers to allow precise, multi-channel phase-shifting of the 40kHz signals to steer the acoustic focus point.
